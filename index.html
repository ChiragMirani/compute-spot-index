Investigating the spot price of AI compute.

Daily public rental offers by chip type, summarized into a compact price index for valuing scarce graphics processor capacity and interpreting data-center buildout economics.

Current public rental prices suggest that for a large frontier model trainer with sustained demand, building or leasing dedicated data center capacity can be economically preferable to renting graphics processors at spot-market prices. The case is strongest when utilization is high, financing is available, and the organization can keep the cluster busy with training, post-training, inference, and experiments; it is weaker for short-lived or uncertain workloads because graphics processors depreciate quickly and rental prices can fall as supply expands.
